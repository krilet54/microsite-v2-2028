Landing Page vs Website: What Does an Indian Startup Actually Need?

Direct answer: they are different tools for different jobs. Choosing the wrong one at the wrong time is one of the most expensive mistakes founders make.

What Is the Actual Difference?

A landing page is a single-purpose page with one goal, minimal navigation and messaging designed to convert a specific audience. A website is multi-page, serves multiple audiences and goals, and is discoverable over time (SEO). Use this analogy: a landing page is a test-lane; a website is the headquarters.

When You Need Just a Landing Page

Use a landing page when you want to validate an idea, run a specific ad campaign, collect a waitlist, or test a single offer. If you have one campaign and one conversion goal, a targeted landing page will outperform a generic homepage every time.

When You Need a Full Website

Build a full website when you are pitching investors, serve multiple audiences, need SEO, or when enterprise clients will look you up. A website is your permanent digital HQ and supports long-term brand and trust building.

When You Need Both — And Why

Most funded startups need both: a core website for credibility and landing pages for paid campaigns. The architecture is simple: website = base, landing pages = campaigns that drive discrete conversions.

The Most Expensive Mistake Founders Make

Building a 15-page site when a 1-page landing page would prove demand first, or sending paid ads to a homepage without a clear conversion path. Both waste months and ad budget.

The Microsite Studio Recommendation

For most founders: a 5–6 page core website (home, about, services, contact, 1–2 service pages) plus 1–2 targeted landing pages for paid campaigns. This combo is economical and fast—often live in 10 days.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is better for a startup — a landing page or website?

It depends on stage. For quick validation and ads, a landing page. For credibility, SEO and multiple audiences, a website.

How much does a landing page cost in India?

From ₹10,000–40,000 depending on design and copy. Simple template pages can be cheaper.

Can a landing page replace a website?

Temporarily yes for testing and campaigns, but not for long-term trust or SEO.

Do I need a website to run Google ads in India?

No — but you should direct ads to a dedicated landing page designed to convert, not a generic homepage.

What should be on a startup landing page?

One clear headline, problem statement, benefit, social proof, a single CTA and a short form or booking link.
