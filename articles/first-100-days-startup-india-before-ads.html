The First 100 Days: What Indian Founders Must Do Before Running a Single Rupee in Ads

Most founders burn their first marketing budget because the website, funnel and messaging weren’t ready. Ads are the final step — not the first. This is the infrastructure checklist for the first 100 days.

Why Ads Fail Without Infrastructure

Ads fail because they send traffic to pages that don’t convert, messages that are vague, and funnels with no follow-up. Without tracking and a CRM, leads go cold. The ad may drive clicks but not customers.

The 4 Infrastructure Layers Every Startup Needs Before Ads

Layer 1 — Positioning

One sentence naming the customer, the problem, and your solution. It should be testable and specific.

Layer 2 — Website/Landing page

A conversion-focused page with clear CTA and social proof.

Layer 3 — Funnel

What happens after a click? Define pages, emails, and follow-up.

Layer 4 — Tracking

GA4, Meta Pixel, and event tracking for conversions and remarketing.

The 100-Day Checklist

Days 1–30: Foundation

  • Nail your positioning statement
  • Build a conversion-focused website
  • Set up GA4 and Meta Pixel
  • Set up basic CRM (HubSpot free)
  • Create one lead magnet or offer to test

Days 31–60: Organic First

  • Publish 2 blog posts per week
  • Post on LinkedIn 3x per week
  • Send cold LinkedIn DM to 20 targeted founders per week
  • Track messages and responses

Days 61–100: Paid Amplification

  • Allocate ₹500–1,000/day to ads that amplify validated messaging
  • Run ads to dedicated landing pages
  • Track cost-per-lead and conversion

The Right Order of Operations — and Why Order Matters

Each layer enables the next: positioning defines the page, the page enables the funnel, and the funnel validates paid spend. Skipping steps leads to wasted ad spend.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should a startup start running ads in India?

When you have a conversion-ready landing page, basic tracking, and a follow-up process.

What should a startup do before digital marketing?

Define positioning, build a conversion-focused page, set up tracking and a CRM.

How much should an Indian startup spend on digital marketing?

Start small — ₹500–1,000/day for tests after validating messaging.

What is the best first marketing channel for Indian startups?

Organic content and targeted outreach (LinkedIn) often provide the best early signals.

How do Indian startups get their first 100 customers?

By combining targeted outreach, a clear value proposition, and conversion-focused landing pages iterated quickly.
