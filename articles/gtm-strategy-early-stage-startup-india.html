GTM Strategy for Early-Stage Indian Startups: A Practical 90-Day Framework

A go-to-market (GTM) strategy is the plan that connects product, message and distribution to find paying customers. Most Indian startups go to market too early or without a clear funnel, wasting budget. This article gives a specific 90-day plan any founder can follow to get first traction.

Why Most Indian Startups Go to Market Wrong

Three common mistakes repeat across founders. First: launching before positioning is clear — teams spend on ads without a single sentence that explains who the product is for and why it matters. Second: spending on advertising before a funnel exists — ads send traffic to homepages that don’t convert. Third: building for everyone instead of one specific customer — the result is diluted messaging and low conversion.

Examples: a SaaS founder runs Facebook ads to a generic site hoping for conversions; a marketplace spends on influencers before testing supply; a B2B product hires sales before the ICP and pricing are validated. These choices cost time and money.

The 3 Questions Every Founder Must Answer Before Spending on Marketing

Question 1 — Who specifically is the first customer? Be precise: 'D2C founders in Delhi with ₹5–50L seed funding' is better than 'SMBs'. Question 2 — What is the one problem we solve better than anyone? Focus on a single clear benefit. Question 3 — What does a conversion look like in the first 90 days? Define the metric: paid trial, demo booked, paid invoice.

Walk through examples: if the first customer is 'small restaurants in Bengaluru', tailor messaging, landing page, outreach, and PPC to that group. Define expected conversion rates and acceptable CAC before scaling.

The 90-Day GTM Framework for Indian Startups

Days 1–30: Positioning and foundation

Nail your message: create a 2-line positioning that names your customer and the problem. Build a 3–5 page conversion-focused website, set up Google Analytics 4 and Meta Pixel, and create a clear lead capture flow with a basic CRM (HubSpot free is fine). Produce one lead magnet or offer to test.

Days 31–60: First distribution

Start with low-cost, high-intent channels: LinkedIn outreach, targeted email lists, and content. Publish 3 pieces of targeted content per week that demonstrate problem/solution/proof. Run outreach campaigns to 20–50 specifically targeted prospects per week and record responses. Use landing pages per campaign.

Days 61–90: Paid amplification

Once organic signals confirm the messaging, allocate a small daily ad budget (₹300–₹500/day) to amplify best-performing landing pages. Track conversions (not clicks). Use the paid traffic to build retargeting audiences and refine copy. Scale cautiously as cost-per-lead stabilizes.

Common GTM Mistakes That Kill Early Traction

Running ads without a funnel, hiring a full team before product-market fit, building a 20-page website when a 3-page site would convert better, or delegating strategy to vendors who don't understand your customer. These errors waste runway and obscure learning.

When to Bring in External Help

DIY until you have evidence: if your early outreach produces repeatable responses and a strong conversion signal, bring in a GTM partner to scale. Frequently Asked Questions

What does GTM strategy mean for a startup?

It’s the plan that aligns product, pricing, message and distribution to acquire customers. It answers who the first customers are, what the single conversion event is, and how to reach them.

How long does it take to build a GTM strategy in India?

A basic, testable GTM plan can be built in 2–4 weeks. Full strategy and execution readiness often takes 6–12 weeks depending on resources and validation speed.

What is the cost of GTM consulting in India?

Costs vary: freelance consultants may charge ₹20,000–80,000 for short engagements; focused agencies or studios can charge from ₹40,000 for a compact engagement that includes strategy and execution support.

Should a startup have a GTM strategy before building the product?

Ideally yes: a minimal GTM hypothesis helps you build the right features and prioritize product-market fit testing early.

What is the difference between GTM strategy and marketing strategy?

GTM ties product, pricing and distribution together — it’s broader than marketing strategy, which focuses specifically on messaging, channels and campaigns.
