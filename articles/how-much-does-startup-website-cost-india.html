How Much Does a Startup Website Cost in India? (The Honest 2026 Answer)

The short answer: a startup website in India can legitimately cost anywhere from ₹5,000 to ₹5,00,000. Both numbers are real — the difference is what you get for the money and what your startup actually needs at its current stage.

Why Startup Website Costs in India Vary So Much

There are three main variables that drive cost. First: who builds it. A DIY builder or template will be cheapest, freelancers are mid-range, and agencies bring strategy, process and QA but cost more. Second: the platform — WordPress, Webflow, Shopify or a fully custom site — each carries different development and hosting costs. Third: scope — whether you're buying a single landing page or a conversion-focused package with copy, SEO, analytics and CRM integrations. These three levers combine into wildly different price outcomes.

For founders, the practical impact of these variables is simple: cheaper isn't always worse if you know what to skip, and expensive isn't always better unless the extra cost buys measurable outcomes like conversions or faster time-to-revenue.

What You Actually Get at Each Price Point

₹5,000–15,000 — DIY builder, template, no strategy

What it is: A basic single-page site or simple multi-section landing page built on a template (Wix, Squarespace, or a low-cost WordPress template). Typically includes hosting for a year, a template license, and lightweight customization (logo, colors, some copy). Time to market: same day to a week.

What's included: Template setup, basic images, contact form (often via third-party), mobile responsiveness. What's missing: professional copy, conversion optimization, tracking, CRM, or ongoing maintenance. Expect DIY support to be ad-hoc. Good for testing an idea or running an early campaign.

₹20,000–50,000 — Freelancer or small agency, basic custom site

What it is: A seven–ten page WordPress or Webflow site built by a freelancer or small agency. Includes a modest amount of custom design, layout work, and basic SEO setup. Time to market: 1–3 weeks.

What's included: Custom templates, simple copy edits, image sourcing, contact forms tied to email, and basic Google Analytics setup. What's missing: a clear conversion-focused strategy, CRO, or integrated CRM automation. Support is usually limited to a short warranty period.

₹50,000–1,50,000 — Professional agency, strategy, conversion-focused

What it is: A strategy-led build by a professional agency. Deliverables typically include brand positioning, messaging, wireframes, design, copy, development, analytics, and QA. Time to market: 2–5 weeks depending on scope.

What's included: Discovery workshop, conversion-focused page templates, SEO basics, analytics and events, and optional lightweight CRM integration. What's missing: heavy custom engineering, enterprise integrations, or a full marketing retainer (sold separately). This tier is the sweet spot for many early-stage startups.

₹1,50,000–5,00,000 — Enterprise-grade, full custom, multi-page

What it is: Fully custom design and engineering for large startups or companies needing advanced integrations, heavy performance optimization, or complex flows. Time to market: 2–3 months or more.

What's included: Full UX research, custom backend work, performance and security hardening, integrations with multiple third-party platforms, and ongoing SLA-based support. What's missing: rarely anything — you pay for completeness and polish.

The Hidden Costs Nobody Tells Founders About

Beyond the headline build cost, founders must budget for:

  • Domain registration: ₹800–2,000/year depending on TLD and privacy options.
  • Hosting: ₹3,000–15,000/year for reliable managed hosting; enterprise or high-traffic hosting costs more.
  • SSL: often included, but premium certs or extended validation add cost.
  • Maintenance & updates: security patches, plugin updates — ₹6,000–30,000/year if outsourced.
  • Integrations: CRM, email marketing, analytics, and chat — initial setup and ongoing fees can add ₹5,000–40,000/year.

Realistic first-year total (for a quality small-agency site): build ₹60,000 + hosting/maintenance/integrations ₹15,000–40,000 = ₹75,000–1,00,000.

What a Founder-Stage Startup Actually Needs

Opinion: most pre-revenue founders do not need a 20-page brochure site. They need a conversion-focused, well-written 4–6 page site with a clear positioning statement, social proof, and a single primary CTA. Speed to market is critical: you want to test messaging, capture leads, and iterate quickly. A focused site with strong positioning paired with one or two campaign landing pages will beat a large, unfocused site every time.

If you’re early-stage, invest in a site that helps you validate offers and acquire first customers — not in unnecessary pages or features that don’t change conversion rates.

The Real Question Isn't Cost — It's ROI

Reframe the decision: the right metric is return on investment. A ₹40,000 site that brings one paying client per month is cheaper than a ₹10,000 site that brings none. Ask: will this site convert the people who land on it? If the answer is yes, it’s worth the price.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a basic startup website cost in India?

Basic sites can start at ₹5,000 (template + DIY) and commonly range ₹20,000–50,000 for a freelancer-built site with modest customization. The right choice depends on speed-to-market and conversion needs.

Is WordPress good for a startup website in India?

Yes — WordPress is versatile, cost-effective, and great for content-first sites. It requires some maintenance and security vigilance, but it’s the most flexible option for scaling content and SEO.

How long does it take to build a startup website in India?

Anywhere from a day (template) to 10 days (Launch Sprint-style agency builds) up to several months for enterprise custom builds. Realistically, a solid 4–6 page site from a competent vendor takes 1–3 weeks.

What is the cheapest way to build a startup website in India?

Using a DIY website builder (Wix, Squarespace) or a low-cost freelancer to set up a template is the cheapest route. Be cautious: migration and scaling costs can be significant later.

Should a startup invest in a website before getting customers?

Yes, but invest proportionally. You need a minimal conversion-ready site to validate and acquire your first customers. Don’t overspend before you’ve proven demand.
