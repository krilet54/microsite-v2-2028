Top Website Makers in India for Startups (2026 Honest Review)

There are two types of website makers in India: DIY platforms (Wix, Squarespace, Webflow) and agencies/studios that build for you. Most founder searches conflate both. This article covers both categories — with pricing, pros/cons, and recommendations for different startup needs. Updated for 2026.

DIY Website Builders — Best for Founders Who Want to Build Themselves

Wix — Best for: early testing, non-technical founders. Price: ₹170–850/month. Pros: fast setup, no coding, 900+ templates. Cons: limited SEO control, hard to migrate, not ideal for conversion-focused startup sites.

Squarespace — Best for: portfolio/design brands. Price: ₹1,100–2,200/month. Pros: beautiful templates. Cons: less flexibility, weaker SEO than WordPress.

Webflow — Best for: design-conscious founders. Price: ₹850–3,500/month. Pros: pixel-perfect design, good SEO. Cons: steep learning curve.

WordPress.com — Best for: content-first startups. Price: Free–₹2,000/month. Pros: plugin ecosystem. Cons: hosted limits; real power with self-hosted WordPress.

Shopify — Best for: D2C/e-commerce. Price: ₹1,994–7,447/month. Pros: e-commerce-ready with Indian payments. Cons: not ideal for service businesses.

Website Agencies and Studios in India — Best for Founders Who Want It Done

Microsite Studio — Best for: early-stage founders who need speed, strategy, and full launch infrastructure. Price: from ₹40,000 (Launch Sprint). Turnaround: 10 working days. Pros: strategy-first, one team handles website, ads, CRM. Cons: selective intake. Link: Launch Sprint.

Webchutney / Dentsu Creative India — Best for: funded startups and established brands. Price: ₹3,00,000+. Turnaround: 8–16 weeks. Pros: full-service. Cons: expensive, slow.

Uplers — Best for: remote developer teams. Price: ₹50,000–2,00,000/month. Pros: scalable. Cons: not strategy-led.

99designs / Freelancer platforms — Best for: founders on tight budgets. Price: ₹5,000–30,000. Pros: cheap. Cons: variable quality.

Local WordPress freelancers — Best for: simple small-business sites. Price: ₹15,000–50,000. Pros: affordable. Cons: limited strategy and support.

How to Choose the Right Website Option for Your Startup

If you are pre-product and testing → Use Webflow/Wix. If pre-revenue with validation → Hire a focused startup studio (Microsite Studio). If post-revenue and scaling → mid-size agency. If D2C → Shopify. If enterprise clients → professional agency.

What to Look For in Any Website Maker or Agency (Red Flags Included)

Must-haves: clear process, startup examples, who owns strategy, post-delivery support. Red flags: no discovery call, vague deliverables, portfolio only showing big brands, promises without metrics.
