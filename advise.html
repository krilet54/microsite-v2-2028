04 — Advise

Before you execute,
you need the
right strategy.

For founders at an inflection point — launching a new product, entering a new market, preparing to raise — who need rigorous strategic thinking, not more output.

GTM ADVISORY · NOT CONSULTING AS USUAL
What This Is
Not consulting in the
traditional sense.

We don't hand you a 60-page strategy document and wish you luck. We work through your specific situation together — the target segment, the positioning, the distribution channels, the pricing — and give you something concrete at the end.

"A strategy you can execute, not admire."

This is for founders who have already figured out the product. Now they need to figure out the market.

DELIVERABLES · SEVEN OUTPUTS · 2–3 WEEKS
What's Included
Seven deliverables.
Two to three weeks.
01
Three deep-dive strategy sessions (2 hours each)

Session 1: Situation analysis — where you are, where you're going, what's blocking you. Session 2: Strategy development — target segment, positioning, channel selection, pricing. Session 3: GTM plan — 90-day launch plan with specific actions, owners, and metrics.

02
Go-to-market strategy document

Target segment definition, positioning statement, channel selection rationale, 90-day execution plan. Written for you, not for a generic audience.

03
Tech stack and infrastructure recommendation

What to build, what to buy, what to integrate — specific to your situation and stage. No generic software advice.

04
Distribution channel analysis

Where your customers actually are and how to reach them cost-effectively. Not where the playbook says they should be.

05
Pricing and packaging recommendation

How to structure your offer for maximum conversion and healthy unit economics. The most underrated lever in early-stage growth.

06
Investor narrative review (if applicable)

If you're raising, we review how your GTM story lands with investors and suggest specific improvements.

07
30-day async support

Email access to your strategist for 30 days after delivery for follow-up questions and iteration. The thinking doesn't stop when the document is done.
